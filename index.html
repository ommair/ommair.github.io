Research Overview

First-principles water force fields & liquid-state anomalies

My core research develops many-body potential energy surfaces (PES) for water that explicitly include 2-body, 3-body, and polarization terms, parameterized to CCSD(T), SAPT, and DFT reference data. These models are implemented in a custom MD engine using quaternion-based rigid-body dynamics, enabling systematic studies of equation-of-state behavior, density anomalies, and structural changes across temperature and pressure.

I refit and analyze three-body (3B) contributions—especially exchange and induction—using short-range and long-range components connected by smooth switching functions. This allows quantitative assessment of how far 3B effects extend in liquid water and how cutoff choices impact predicted properties.

Three-body interactions & long-range damping

A key part of my work is characterizing the spatial range and functional form of three-body contributions in liquid water:

  • Fitting short- and long-range 3B PES components to extensive trimer datasets.
  • Designing smooth long-range damping that preserves physical behavior while controlling cost.
  • Quantifying the impact of 3B cutoffs on density, radial distribution functions, and energetics.

Rigid & flexible water models with classical and path-integral MD

Beyond rigid water, I implement fully flexible, first-principles water potentials in i-Pi to include nuclear quantum effects via path-integral MD:

  • Evaluating flexibility and quantum effects on RDFs, diffusion, and related observables.
  • Comparing rigid vs flexible models for bulk and supercooled liquid water.
  • Generalizing these techniques toward biomolecular and materials applications.

Crystal Structure Prediction (CSP)

I contributed to the CCDC 7th Blind Test of Crystal Structure Prediction (BT7) using intermonomer force fields derived from SAPT and UPACK-based CSP workflows. This work demonstrates how high-level quantum chemistry–based force fields can improve structure ranking for flexible agrochemical molecules.

Plasma and cosmology projects

Earlier research includes plasma-physics studies of dust-acoustic solitary waves and electromagnetic ion cyclotron waves in the Earth’s magnetosphere, as well as work on hybrid inflation with chaotic potentials in precision cosmology.

Software & Computational Tools

CCpol23+ Rigid-Water MD (Quaternion-Based Rigid Body Dynamics)

I maintain an open-source Fortran molecular dynamics engine implementing the rigid CCpol23+ water model with explicit 2-body, 3-body, and polarization terms. Key features include:

  • Quaternion-based rigid-body integration for NVE, NVT, and NPT ensembles.
  • Ewald electrostatics, polarization/induction, and long-range-damped 3-body interactions.
  • HPC-oriented workflows with example systems spanning ambient to supercooled conditions.
Zenodo record GitHub repository

Analysis & workflow automation

  • Python scripts for RDFs, diffusion coefficients, dielectric constants, and error metrics.
  • Data-pipeline tools for parsing MD outputs and aggregating large simulation campaigns.
  • Parameter-fitting utilities for many-body force fields using energies, forces, and trimer datasets.

Interfaces & ecosystem

  • Interfacing custom potentials with DL_POLY, LAMMPS, and i-Pi.
  • Experience with GROMACS, VASP, Quantum ESPRESSO, ORCA, MERCURY, and UPACK.
  • Use of machine-learning and data-science tools (scikit-learn, pandas, NumPy) for model analysis.
